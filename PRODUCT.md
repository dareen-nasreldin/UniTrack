# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A Computer Engineering student — one person, used daily. Context: managing coursework deadlines, assignment queues, and internship application pipelines. They are technical, pattern-aware, and intolerant of friction. They open this app when they have something to capture or a backlog to process; they want the tool to get out of the way.

## Product Purpose

UniTrack is a personal task tracker built around an inbox-first workflow: capture fast, organize deliberately. Tasks flow Inbox → Pending → Done. It is not a team tool, not a project management suite — it is a sharp, single-user system for someone who knows what they need.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, dense, engineering-grade. Confident and precise. The design should feel like a well-made personal tool, not a product trying to sell itself. No fluff, no gamification, no decorative chrome — every element either carries information or creates useful structure.

The landing page is the exception: it can be bolder and more typographically committed, functioning as a personal manifesto rather than a SaaS pitch.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS** (Asana, Monday.com): pastel gradients, rounded blobs, "empower your workflow" copy, feature-highlight carousels.
- **Cute productivity apps** (Todoist, TickTick): gamification, celebration confetti, friendly illustrations, encouraging microcopy. Wrong register entirely.

## Design Principles

1. **Information over decoration** — if an element doesn't carry meaning, it doesn't belong.
2. **Capture is sacred** — the quick-note path must be the fastest thing in the UI; never make it feel like a form.
3. **Confidence through density** — well-organized tight layouts read as competent; sparse padding reads as filler.
4. **The landing tells a story; the app tells the time** — the landing page can have voice and weight; the app interior should be transparent infrastructure.
5. **Fail visibly, recover cheaply** — errors and empty states are first-class citizens, not afterthoughts.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the floor. Skip link and ARIA labels already in place. No known specific accommodation needs beyond standard contrast and keyboard navigation support.
